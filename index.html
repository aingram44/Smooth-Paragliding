What to Expect

Take off from any open field and fly. You'll cruise at 30mph and go up to 15,000ft in altitude. You can fly for hours on a few gallons of gas and just enjoy the beautiful view from above.

You don't need a license or a permit. Yet, training is highly recommended. Danger is at the experience of the pilots ability to understand the current weather and limits of the paragliding equipment.

Cruising up to desired altitude, you can kill your engine and glide all the way down. Yes, you can also restart your engine from midair.

Getting Started

Full rigs can start at $10k retail. You need the paramotor and parachute. Depending on the weather conditions, having gloves and layers of clothing might be needed. The constant wind during flight, plus the elevation of altitude, you can get cold during flights.

Training averages around $2.5k. Some training you'll have a 15 flights due in 60 days before you are considered ready for flight. With the training, some trainers let you use their equipment to learn on.
